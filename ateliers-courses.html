Her Story

Born in Sydney Australia, Kristin came into contact, from an early age, with Australian art through her parents. She was exposed to a wide range of artists and lived with modern art as part of family life. She remembers fervent discussions about art and life around the kitchen table and going to exhibitions from the age of four.

At 14, her father took the family to live in France where they rented a large house in Croissy sur Seine. Kristin went to school, learned to speak French and studied ballet at the Moulin Rouge. Coburn’s formative experiences growing up in her family, and with her father, the renowned Australian artist John Coburn, are the basis for her relationship with art. (John Coburn paintings)

Coburn senior went to France to oversee the weaving of the Opera House Tapestries. While in Europe family holidays included detours to some of the many Art Museums to take in treasures by Van Eyck, Rembrandt, Velasquez and Matisse.

Kristin formally studied art in her teens at East Sydney Technical College, which evolved into Alexander Mackie College and is now COFA. This resulted in a passion for painting and printmaking and she went on to work as a master printmaker, printing limited edition prints for some of Australia’s finest artists including John Olsen, Martin Sharp and Gary Shead. She returned to painting in the late 90’s, going back to The National Art School in 2002 as a way to refocus and refine her skills. Kristin has learnt art formally and informally from diverse artists including her father, Margaret Olley, Kevin Connor, Wallace Thornton, Charles Blackman and Lloyd Rees.

Her work includes Still Life, Interiors and landscapes. Kristin has been hung in the Portia Geach Prize for Portraiture and in the Blake Prize for Religious Art and touring exhibition. She exhibits in galleries around the country and has regular solo exhibitions in the Landship Gallery at Middle Head.
